Mission

Rwanda needs builders, not certificate collectors.

This is a small, demanding academy for engineers who can already code and now need the discipline to ship useful software, telecom products, and practical tools to real people.

The user must exist before the product does.

Every serious idea must survive contact with a real Rwandan user, not just a convincing pitch.
Design for the device people already use

Smartphones, voice, SMS, USSD, and lightweight web flows matter more than shiny dashboards.

Kinyarwanda is a product requirement

Language access is part of the system design, especially for rural users.

Entrepreneurship is the output

The goal is a working product, a reachable user, and evidence that the problem is worth building for.

Program arc

Train, prove, build.

The academy is structured like a product pipeline. You do not move forward because the course ended. You move forward because the evidence improved.

01 / TRAINING

Product engineering with modern tools

Prototype, design, build, deploy, and support with practical engineering habits, telecom awareness, and AI-assisted workflows where they create leverage. The work happens on real project ideas, not toy exercises.

Weeks 1-8 · remote · English
02 / GATE

A user conversation, not a vibe

Submit a field visit report and proof of one specific person affected by the problem. Mobayilo's local partner network evaluates the field signal, and the Academy reviews the product and technical path.

Selection by evidence · no fixed quota
03 / PRODUCT

Three months to make it real

Selected builders receive tool support and continue toward deployed products, portfolio visibility, investor/NGO introductions, and future mentor responsibility.

3 months · beyond MVP

Apply

Cohort 1 applications are open.

There are no trick questions. More concrete answers make a stronger application.

What the application is testing

We are not looking for perfect English or polished startup language. We are looking for real answers, evidence, and commitment.

  • What problem have you personally observed in your community?
  • Who suffers from it? Describe one specific person.
  • What have you built that runs?
  • What are you willing to give up for 6-8 focused weeks?

Application questions

Open each question before you apply. Strong answers are concrete, honest, and tied to something you have seen or built.

What problem have you personally observed in your community?

Name the place, the situation, and the people affected. The strongest answer sounds like field notes, not a startup slogan.

Example shape: "In [place], [person/group] struggles with [specific situation] because [constraint]. I saw this when..."
Who suffers from this problem? Describe one specific person.

Write about one person, not a broad category. A name, age, job, location, routine, or conversation makes the problem real.

We want to understand the future user before the product exists.
What have you built that runs?

Share a GitHub repo, deployed project, school project, script, prototype, or anything functional. Rough is fine. Running matters.

We are not judging polish. We are looking for proof that you can ship something.
What are you willing to give up for 6-8 focused weeks?

Be honest about the real cost. Time, habits, side projects, job search rhythm, or social commitments all count.

Commitment is easier to trust when it names the tradeoff.
