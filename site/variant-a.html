Ship real software to a real Rwandan user.

"mobayilo" = mobile telephone, in Kinyarwanda. The device is the market.

8 weeks of AI-assisted engineering. A field visit. One real conversation with one real user. The strongest ideas advance to a funded 3-month product phase.

:~$ ./apply.sh --cohort=1

premise

Africa-first. Not a comparison.

Rwandan engineers solving Rwandan problems, on their own terms. Progress measured against your last commit — not someone else's stack.

constraint The user must exist before the product does.

constraint If your user lives in a village, your product speaks Kinyarwanda. Voice, USSD, SMS — designed in, not bolted on.

cohort-1.spec

program --phases

● phase[1] · weeks 1–8 · training
SDLC with AI: prototype → design → build → deploy → support. Claude Code, not prompt tips. Remote. English.
● phase[2] · gate · field-report
Submit: a field visit report and proof of one real conversation with a future user. The Kigali partner evaluates. No quota — evidence decides.
● phase[3] · months 1–3 · product
Funded build: a Pro AI subscription for 3 months. Ship beyond a basic MVP. Land in the Academy portfolio. Mentor cohort 2.

curriculum --list

Engineering discipline, not chatbot tricks.

[01] prototype  — state the question; smallest thing that answers it
[02] design     — data shape, user shape, failure shape
[03] build      — AI-assisted, but you own every line
[04] deploy     — ship to one user, not to staging
[05] support    — the product isn't done until someone keeps using it

apply

There are no trick questions. The more concrete your answers, the stronger your application.

What problem have you personally observed in your community that technology hasn't solved yet? Name the place, the situation, the people.
Who suffers from it? One specific person — name, age, situation. If you've met them, tell us.
A GitHub repo, a deployed project, anything that runs. We're not grading code — we want proof you ship.
What are you willing to give up for 6–8 weeks? Be honest. We want to know you've thought about the real cost.
