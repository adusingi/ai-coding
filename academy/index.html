// 01 mission

The user must exist before the product does.

Smartphone penetration is now widespread across Rwanda, including rural areas. The opportunity to build digital solutions for Rwandan problems — designed with Rwanda's market, culture, and language in mind — is larger than it has ever been. We are not comparing Rwanda to anywhere else. Rwandan engineers solving Rwandan problems on their own terms.

[01]

Africa-first

Not a generic AI bootcamp translated. The brief is local: a user you've actually met, a problem you've actually seen.

[02]

Kinyarwanda by design

Voice, USSD, SMS, Kinyarwanda interfaces — the language gap is a design challenge to be proud of, not an afterthought.

[03]

Ship or it didn't happen

The work speaks for itself. You progress by shipping something a real Rwandan user can open on their phone today.

“Train engineers to build with AI, hold them accountable to a real Rwandan user, and let the work speak for itself.”
— Aimable Dusingizimana, founder
// 02 program arc

Three phases. One outcome: a shipped product.

Selection is fluid. Idea quality and evidence determine who passes the gate — not a fixed quota.

PHASE 01

Training

Intensive remote training in AI-assisted engineering. Claude Code and the full SDLC — Prototype → Design → Build → Deploy → Support. English instruction. Live sessions, not a self-serve LMS.

8 weeks
GATE

Project selection

Submit a field visit report and proof of at least one real conversation with a future user. The Kigali-based partner evaluates ideas. Aimable confirms. The user must exist.

checkpoint
PHASE 02

Product phase

Selected participants receive a $20/month Claude or Codex Pro subscription for 3 months to build a working product beyond a basic MVP — and deploy it to the user you named at the gate.

3 months
AFTER

Portfolio & mentorship

Products enter the Academy portfolio — promoted to investors, NGOs, and government programs. Graduates become mentors for the next cohort. The flywheel turns.

ongoing

// 04 apply

Four questions. No trick questions.

› We are not looking for the right words. We are looking for real answers. The more detail and honesty you bring, the stronger your application. Nothing here eliminates you — everything here helps us understand you.
// question 01 — the problem
What problem have you personally observed in your community that technology hasn't solved yet?
Be specific. Name the place, the situation, the people affected.
// question 02 — the person
Who suffers from this problem? Describe one specific person — not a category of people.
A name, an age, a situation. If you've met them, tell us.
// question 03 — something you've built
Share something you've built — a GitHub repo, a deployed project, anything that runs.
We're not evaluating code quality. We want proof you can ship something that works.
// question 04 — the commitment
What are you willing to give up for 6–8 weeks to focus on this program?
Be honest. We want to know you've thought about the real cost.
your answers go directly to Aimable.
